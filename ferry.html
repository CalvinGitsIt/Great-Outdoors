A Ferry Expedition


Hours: The Washington State Ferry to Bainbridge Island operates year-round with frequent sailings throughout the day. Ferries typically run every 30 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the time of day and season. It's recommended to check the specific schedule on the Washington State Ferries website for up-to-date times.

Requirements: No special requirements are needed to board the ferry, but passengers must pay the fare, which varies depending on whether you are traveling as a walk-on passenger or in a vehicle. Tickets can be purchased at the terminal or online in advance. Passengers are encouraged to arrive early, especially during peak travel times.

Accessibility: The ferry is wheelchair accessible, with ramps and elevators to accommodate passengers with disabilities. Accessible restrooms are also available on board. Passengers with mobility needs are encouraged to inform the ferry staff for assistance if necessary.

Parking availability: Parking is available at several lots near the Bainbridge Island Ferry terminal. The WSF terminal parking lot, managed by U-Park, is located directly outside the terminal entrance, with daily rates ranging from $7 (evening) to $15 (24 hours). Additional lots operated by Diamond Parking are within two blocks of the terminal, such as Lot D508 with 465 spaces and Lot D505 off Winslow Way. Free Park & Ride options are also available through Kitsap Transit at locations like American Legion Post and Bethany Lutheran Church, with bus routes connecting to the terminal.

Fun fact: The ferry ride from Seattle to Bainbridge Island offers stunning views of the Puget Sound and the Seattle skyline. The trip takes about 35 minutes and is a popular way for tourists and locals alike to experience the beauty of the Pacific Northwest. Bainbridge Island itself is known for its charming small-town atmosphere, with boutique shops, restaurants, and beautiful parks.
