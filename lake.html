Requirements: No special permits are typically required to enjoy the public areas of Moses Lake, but some activities, like boating or fishing, may require a license or fee. Visitors are encouraged to follow local regulations, especially around water activities and park usage.

Accessibility: Moses Lake has several accessible features, including ADA-compliant restrooms and parking areas at various parks and recreation sites around the lake. Parking availability Parking is available at various locations around Moses Lake. Public parking lots can be found across Balsam Street from the Civic Center, with handicapped parking available in the police department parking lot next to the Civic Center. For boat owners, there are parking options for trailers, including hourly, daily, weekly, and monthly rates through services like Truck Parking Club. It's advisable to check specific locations for parking availability, especially during peak seasons or events.

Fun fact: Moses Lake was originally a large glacial lake and is named after a Native American chief, Moses, who lived in the area. The lake is a popular destination for water sports such as boating, jet skiing, and fishing. It also hosts the annual Moses Lake Airshow, one of the largest airshows in Washington, attracting aviation enthusiasts from all over.
