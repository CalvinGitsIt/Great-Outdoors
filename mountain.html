Climb the Mountain!

Mount Rainier: Hours: Mount Rainier National Park is open year-round, 24 hours a day. However, certain facilities or roads may be closed seasonally due to snow or weather conditions. Visitors should check for road closures or specific facility hours before visiting. Requirements: Visitors to Mount Rainier National Park are encouraged to follow Leave No Trace principles, stay on designated trails, and follow park rules for safety. For those planning to hike or summit the mountain, certain permits or passes may be required, especially for climbing. More information on specific passes can be found here. Accessibility: Mount Rainier National Park has accessible features for visitors with disabilities, including designated parking spaces, accessible restrooms, and some paved trails like the Grove of the Patriarchs Trail. However, many of the park's more rugged areas may not be fully accessible due to the terrain. Fun Fact: Mount Rainier is an active stratovolcano and is considered one of the most dangerous volcanoes in the United States due to its potential for eruption and proximity to populated areas. It also holds the title of the tallest peak in the Cascade Range, standing at 14,411 feet (4,392 meters) above sea level.
